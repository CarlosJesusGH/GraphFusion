Clustering and Enrichment

Select a factor

A factor is a low-dimensional representation of the data, such as a multimodal embedding, a principal component, a t-SNE embedding, or a graphlet degree vector matrix (where the number of rows in the matrix is equal to the number of nodes in the network, and the columns are the Graphlet Degree Vectors, i.e., 11 columns per node representing the coordinates corresponding to the 11 non-redundant orbits). The input file should be a tab-separated matrix without a header or row names. The values should be positive real numbers. The matrix does not need to be square or symmetric.

Enrichments

Enrichment analysis for assessing the functional relevance of the entity clusters obtained by the framework. Enrichments are computed using the annotations file.
Select the annotations file:



The plot shows three types of enrichments: the percentage of clusters having at least one of their entities (e.g. genes) enriched (in blue); the percentage of annotations enriched (in red); and the percentage of entities (e.g. genes) having at least one of their annotations enriched in their clusters over all annotated entities (in purple).
